Our Core Programs

Explore the four primary pillars of our ministry and operations, designed to uplift caregivers and provide holistic growth for children living with disabilities.

Discipleship

Caregiving for a child with a disability can feel like a lonely journey. Our Discipleship program focuses on nurturing the spiritual, mental, and emotional well-being of caregivers and their families.

Through local cell groups, family fellowship meetings, and Christ-centered counseling, we build a community where families find mutual comfort, strong emotional support, and the hope needed to move forward.

Advocacy

We raise our voices to protect the legal, human, and social rights of children living with disabilities. Stigma and ignorance often deny these children access to education, healthcare, and baseline community services.

HBD works with local leaders, civic organizations, and policymakers to champion inclusive policies, ensuring schools and public infrastructure become accessible, and that child protection laws are strictly enforced.

Awareness

Fear and discrimination are born from a lack of understanding. Our Awareness campaigns aim to dismantle myths and misconceptions surrounding child disability.

We organize community workshops, school assemblies, and public campaigns to educate neighborhoods on medical realities and disability-inclusive behaviors. By educating the wider public, we cultivate a culture of empathy, belonging, and active inclusion.

Empowerment

Financial security translates to security for the child. Many caregivers are unable to hold full-time jobs due to intensive caregiving demands. Our Empowerment programs provide the solution.

We train caregivers in practical, home-based skills (such as soap making, tailoring, beadwork, and urban farming) and support them with micro-loans and business management mentoring, helping families achieve financial independence.

Building compassionate communities where every child is valued and every caregiver is supported.
